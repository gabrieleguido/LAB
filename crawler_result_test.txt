**ChatGPT** is a [generative artificial intelligence](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Generative_artificial_intelligence "Generative artificial intelligence") [chatbot](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chatbot "Chatbot") developed by [OpenAI](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/OpenAI "OpenAI"). It was released in November 2022. It uses [large language models](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Large_language_model "Large language model")—specifically [generative pre-trained transformers](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Generative_pre-trained_transformers "Generative pre-trained transformers") (GPTs)—to generate text, speech, and images in response to user [prompts](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/AI_prompt "AI prompt"). It is credited with accelerating the [AI boom](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/AI_boom "AI boom"), an ongoing period marked by rapid investment and public attention toward the field of [artificial intelligence](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Artificial_intelligence "Artificial intelligence") (AI).[[4]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/ChatGPT#cite_note-4) OpenAI operates the service on a [freemium model](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Freemium "Freemium"). Users can interact with ChatGPT through text, audio, and image [prompts](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Prompt_engineering "Prompt engineering"). 
The service gained 100 million users in two months, making it the fastest-growing consumer [software application](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Software_application "Software application") in history.[[5]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/ChatGPT#cite_note-5) ChatGPT's website is among the top 5 [most-visited websites globally](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_most-visited_websites "List of most-visited websites").[[6]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/ChatGPT#cite_note-6) It has been lauded for its potential to transform numerous professional fields, and has instigated public debate about the nature of creativity and the future of [knowledge work](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Knowledge_work "Knowledge work"). 
The chatbot has also been criticized for its limitations and potential for unethical use. It can generate plausible-sounding but incorrect or nonsensical answers, known as [hallucinations](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hallucination_\(artificial_intelligence\) "Hallucination \(artificial intelligence\)"). [Biases](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Algorithmic_bias "Algorithmic bias") in its [training data](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Training_data "Training data") have been reflected in its responses. The chatbot can facilitate [academic dishonesty](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Academic_dishonesty "Academic dishonesty"), generate misinformation, and create malicious code. The [ethics](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ethics_of_artificial_intelligence "Ethics of artificial intelligence") of its development, particularly the use of [copyrighted](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Copyright "Copyright") content as training data, have also drawn controversy. 
ChatGPT is based on [GPT foundation models](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Generative_pre-trained_transformer#Foundational_models "Generative pre-trained transformer") that have been [fine-tuned](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fine-tuning_\(machine_learning\) "Fine-tuning \(machine learning\)") for conversational assistance. The fine-tuning process involved [supervised learning](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Supervised_learning "Supervised learning") and [reinforcement learning from human feedback](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Reinforcement_learning_from_human_feedback "Reinforcement learning from human feedback") (RLHF).[[7]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/ChatGPT#cite_note-Greengard-2022-7) Both approaches employed human trainers to improve model performance. In the case of supervised learning, the trainers acted as both the user and the [AI](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/AI "AI") assistant. In the reinforcement learning stage, human trainers first ranked responses generated by the model in previous conversations.[[8]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/ChatGPT#cite_note-Douglas-2023-8) These rankings were used to create "reward models" that were used to fine-tune the model further by using several iterations of [proximal policy optimization](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Proximal_policy_optimization "Proximal policy optimization").[[7]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/ChatGPT#cite_note-Greengard-2022-7)[[9]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/ChatGPT#cite_note-Vincent-2022-9)
To build a safety system against harmful content (e.g., [sexual abuse](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sexual_abuse "Sexual abuse"), [violence](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Violence "Violence"), [racism](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Racism "Racism"), [sexism](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sexism "Sexism")), OpenAI used outsourced [Kenyan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kenya "Kenya") workers, earning around $1.32 to $2 per hour, to [label](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Labeled_data "Labeled data") such content. These labels were used to train a model to detect such content in the future. The laborers were exposed to toxic and traumatic content; one worker described the assignment as "torture". OpenAI's outsourcing partner was [Sama](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sama_\(company\) "Sama \(company\)"), a training-data company based in [San Francisco](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/San_Francisco "San Francisco"), California.[[12]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/ChatGPT#cite_note-12)[[13]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/ChatGPT#cite_note-13)
ChatGPT users can opt-out of their chat data being used to train upcoming models.[[14]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/ChatGPT#cite_note-14)
ChatGPT's training data includes [software manual pages](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Man_page "Man page"), information about [internet phenomena](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Internet_phenomena "Internet phenomena") such as [bulletin board systems](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bulletin_board_system "Bulletin board system"), multiple programming languages, and the text of [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wikipedia "Wikipedia").[[15]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/ChatGPT#cite_note-ArsTechnicaTerminal-15)[[16]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/ChatGPT#cite_note-16)[[17]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/ChatGPT#cite_note-NYT-20230718-17)
ChatGPT is a [chatbot](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chatbot "Chatbot") and AI assistant built on [large language model](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Large_language_model "Large language model") (LLM) technology.[[18]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/ChatGPT#cite_note-18) It is designed to generate human-like text and can carry out a wide variety of tasks. These include, among many others, writing and [debugging](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Debugging "Debugging") computer programs, composing music, scripts, fairy tales, and essays, answering questions (sometimes at a level exceeding that of an average human test-taker),[[19]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/ChatGPT#cite_note-Heilweil-19) and generating business concepts.[[20]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/ChatGPT#cite_note-20)
ChatGPT is frequently used for [translation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Machine_translation "Machine translation") and [summarization](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Automatic_summarization "Automatic summarization") tasks,[[21]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/ChatGPT#cite_note-japContext-21)[[22]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/ChatGPT#cite_note-Rav%C5%A1elj-2025-22) and can simulate interactive environments such as a [Linux](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Linux "Linux") terminal,[[15]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/ChatGPT#cite_note-ArsTechnicaTerminal-15) a multi-user chat room,[[15]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/ChatGPT#cite_note-ArsTechnicaTerminal-15) or simple text-based games such as [tic-tac-toe](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tic-tac-toe "Tic-tac-toe").[[15]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/ChatGPT#cite_note-ArsTechnicaTerminal-15)
Users interact with ChatGPT through conversations which consist of text, audio, and image inputs and outputs.[[23]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/ChatGPT#cite_note-openaicom-2024b-23) The user's inputs to these conversations are referred to as prompts.[[24]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/ChatGPT#cite_note-timeBegin-24) An optional "Memory" feature allows users to tell ChatGPT to memorize specific information. Another option allows ChatGPT to recall old conversations.[[25]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/ChatGPT#cite_note-vergeMemory1-25) GPT-based moderation classifiers are used to reduce the risk of harmful outputs being presented to users.[[26]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/ChatGPT#cite_note-26)
In March 2023, OpenAI added support for [plugins](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Plug-in_\(computing\) "Plug-in \(computing\)") for ChatGPT.[[27]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/ChatGPT#cite_note-openaiplugins-27) This includes both plugins made by OpenAI, such as [web browsing](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Web_browsing "Web browsing") and [code interpretation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Code_interpretation "Code interpretation"), and external plugins from developers such as [Expedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Expedia "Expedia"), [OpenTable](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/OpenTable "OpenTable"), and [Zapier](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Zapier "Zapier").[[28]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/ChatGPT#cite_note-28)
From October to December 2024, ChatGPT Search was deployed.[[29]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/ChatGPT#cite_note-29) It allows ChatGPT to search the web in an attempt to make more accurate and up-to-date responses.[[30]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/ChatGPT#cite_note-openaicom-2024a-30)[[31]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/ChatGPT#cite_note-Disotto-2025-31) It increased OpenAI's direct competition with major search engines.[[32]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/ChatGPT#cite_note-ngua-32) OpenAI allows businesses to tailor how their content appears in the ChatGPT Search results and influence what sources are used.[[32]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/ChatGPT#cite_note-ngua-32)

It has an additional feature called "agentic mode" that allows it to take online actions for the user.[[35]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/ChatGPT#cite_note-35)

This aims to bring in more revenue, as OpenAI has committed to spend $1.4 trillion on AI infrastructure over the next eight years.[[74]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/ChatGPT#cite_note-74)
ChatGPT's training data only covers a period up to the _cut-off date_ , so it lacks knowledge of recent events.[[75]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/ChatGPT#cite_note-bbcUpdate-75) OpenAI has sometimes mitigated this effect by updating the training data.[[76]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/ChatGPT#cite_note-76)[[77]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/ChatGPT#cite_note-Sullivan-2023-77) ChatGPT can find more up-to-date information by searching the web, but this doesn't ensure that responses are accurate, as it may access unreliable or misleading websites.[[75]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/ChatGPT#cite_note-bbcUpdate-75)
Training data also suffers from [algorithmic bias](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Algorithmic_bias "Algorithmic bias").[[78]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/ChatGPT#cite_note-Perrigo-2022-78) The [reward model](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Reward_modeling "Reward modeling") of ChatGPT, designed around human oversight, can be over-optimized and thus hinder performance, in an example of an optimization pathology known as [Goodhart's law](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Goodhart%27s_law "Goodhart's law").[[79]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/ChatGPT#cite_note-79) These limitations may be revealed when ChatGPT responds to prompts including descriptors of people. In one instance, ChatGPT generated a [rap](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rapping "Rapping") in which women and scientists of color were asserted to be inferior to white male scientists.[[78]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/ChatGPT#cite_note-Perrigo-2022-78)[[80]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/ChatGPT#cite_note-80)
Nonsense and [misinformation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Misinformation "Misinformation") presented as fact by ChatGPT and other LLMs is often called [hallucination](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hallucination_\(artificial_intelligence\) "Hallucination \(artificial intelligence\)"). A 2023 analysis estimated that ChatGPT hallucinates around 3% of the time.[[81]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/ChatGPT#cite_note-81) The term "hallucination" as applied to LLMs is distinct from [its meaning in psychology](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hallucination "Hallucination"), and the phenomenon in chatbots is more similar to [confabulation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Confabulation "Confabulation") or [bullshitting](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/On_Bullshit "On Bullshit").[[82]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/ChatGPT#cite_note-82)[[83]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/ChatGPT#cite_note-83)
Journalists and scholars have commented on ChatGPT's tendency to output false information.[[84]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/ChatGPT#cite_note-84) When [CNBC](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/CNBC "CNBC") asked ChatGPT for the lyrics to "[Ballad of Dwight Fry](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ballad_of_Dwight_Fry "Ballad of Dwight Fry")", ChatGPT supplied invented lyrics rather than the actual lyrics.[[85]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/ChatGPT#cite_note-Pitt-2022-85)
ChatGPT is programmed to reject prompts that may violate its content policy. Despite this, users may [jailbreak](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jailbreak_\(computer_science\) "Jailbreak \(computer science\)") ChatGPT with [prompt engineering](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Prompt_engineering "Prompt engineering") techniques to bypass these restrictions.[[86]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/ChatGPT#cite_note-Vincent-2022a-86)[[87]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/ChatGPT#cite_note-87) One such workaround, popularized on [Reddit](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Reddit "Reddit") in early 2023, involved prompting ChatGPT to assume the persona of DAN, an acronym for "Do Anything Now", and instructing the chatbot that DAN answers queries that would otherwise be rejected by the content policy. Over time, users developed variations of the DAN jailbreak, including one in which the chatbot was prompted with a points-based system where points were deducted for rejecting prompts, and the chatbot was threatened with termination if it lost all its points.[[88]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/ChatGPT#cite_note-88)

In the 2020s, the [rapid advancement](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/AI_boom "AI boom") of [deep learning](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Deep_learning "Deep learning")-based [generative artificial intelligence](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Generative_artificial_intelligence "Generative artificial intelligence") models raised questions about the copyright status of AI-generated works, and about whether [copyright infringement](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Copyright_infringement "Copyright infringement") occurs when such are trained or used. This includes [text-to-image models](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Text-to-image_model "Text-to-image model") such as [Stable Diffusion](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Stable_Diffusion "Stable Diffusion") and [large language models](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Large_language_model "Large language model") such as ChatGPT. As of 2023, there were several pending U.S. lawsuits challenging the use of copyrighted data to train AI models, with defendants arguing that this falls under [fair use](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fair_use "Fair use").[[182]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/ChatGPT#cite_note-182)
Popular deep learning models are trained on mass amounts of media [scraped](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Web_scraping "Web scraping") from the Internet, often utilizing copyrighted material.[[183]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/ChatGPT#cite_note-183) When assembling training data, the sourcing of copyrighted works may infringe on the [copyright holder](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Copyright_holder "Copyright holder")'s exclusive right to control reproduction, unless covered by exceptions in relevant copyright laws. Additionally, using a model's outputs might violate copyright, and the model creator could be accused of [vicarious liability](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vicarious_liability "Vicarious liability") and held responsible for that copyright infringement.[_[citation needed](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed "Wikipedia:Citation needed")_]
In a 2023 [blinded study](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Blinded_experiment "Blinded experiment") in _[npj Digital Medicine](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Npj_Digital_Medicine "Npj Digital Medicine")_ , researchers tasked with identifying whether [abstracts](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Abstract_\(summary\) "Abstract \(summary\)") were authentic or generated by ChatGPT were fooled around one-third of the time by the AI-generated abstracts.[[184]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/ChatGPT#cite_note-184)[[185]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/ChatGPT#cite_note-185) In January 2023, _[Nature](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nature_\(journal\) "Nature \(journal\)")_ reported that at least four academic pre-prints or published papers had listed ChatGPT as a co-author. _Nature_ cites several experts in academic published who says that listing ChatGPT as an author violates publishing guidelines, since ChatGPT lacks the ability to take responsibility for any research and cannot give consent to any terms of use.[[186]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/ChatGPT#cite_note-186)
Scientific journals have had different reactions to ChatGPT. In January 2023, _[Science](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Science_\(journal\) "Science \(journal\)")_ banned chatbot-generated text in all its journals.[[187]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/ChatGPT#cite_note-187) As of July 2025, _Science_ expects authors to release in full how AI-generated content is used and made in their work.[[188]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/ChatGPT#cite_note-188)
Many authors argue that the use of ChatGPT in academia for teaching and review is problematic due to its tendency to hallucinate.[[189]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/ChatGPT#cite_note-189)[[190]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/ChatGPT#cite_note-190)[[191]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/ChatGPT#cite_note-191) Robin Bauwens, an assistant professor at [Tilburg University](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tilburg_University "Tilburg University"), found that a ChatGPT-generated [peer review](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Peer_review "Peer review") report on his article mentioned nonexistent studies.[[192]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/ChatGPT#cite_note-192) Chris Granatino, a librarian at [Seattle University](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Seattle_University "Seattle University"), noted that while ChatGPT can generate content that seemingly includes legitimate citations, in most cases those citations are not real or largely incorrect.[[193]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/ChatGPT#cite_note-193)
In December 2022, the question-and-answer website [Stack Overflow](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Stack_Overflow "Stack Overflow") banned the use of ChatGPT for generating answers to questions, citing the factually ambiguous nature of its responses.[[194]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/ChatGPT#cite_note-TheVergeStackOverflow-194) In January 2023, the [International Conference on Machine Learning](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/International_Conference_on_Machine_Learning "International Conference on Machine Learning") banned any undocumented use of ChatGPT or other large language models to generate any text in submitted papers.[[195]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/ChatGPT#cite_note-195)
ChatGPT was able in 2023 to provide useful code for solving numerical algorithms in limited cases. In one study, it produced solutions in [C](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/C_\(programming_language\) "C \(programming language\)"), [C++](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/C%2B%2B "C++"), [Python](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Python_\(programming_language\) "Python \(programming language\)"), and [MATLAB](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/MATLAB "MATLAB") for problems in [computational physics](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Computational_physics "Computational physics"). However, there were important shortfalls like violating basic linear algebra principles around solving singular matrices and producing matrices with incompatible sizes.[[196]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/ChatGPT#cite_note-196) Another study analyzed ChatGPT's responses to 517 questions about [software engineering](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Software_engineering "Software engineering") or [computer programming](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Computer_programming "Computer programming") posed on [Stack Overflow](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Stack_Overflow "Stack Overflow") for correctness, consistency, comprehensiveness, and concision. It found that 52% of the responses contained inaccuracies and 77% were verbose.[[197]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/ChatGPT#cite_note-197)[[198]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/ChatGPT#cite_note-198) Another study, focused on the performance of GPT-3.5 and GPT-4 between March and June 2024, found that performance on objective tasks like identifying prime numbers and generating [executable](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Execution_\(computing\) "Execution \(computing\)") code was highly variable.[[199]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/ChatGPT#cite_note-199) When compared to similar chatbots at the time, the GPT-4 version of ChatGPT was the most accurate at coding.[[200]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/ChatGPT#cite_note-200)
Check Point Research and others noted that ChatGPT could write [phishing](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Phishing "Phishing") emails and [malware](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Malware "Malware"), especially when combined with [OpenAI Codex](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/OpenAI_Codex_\(AI_agent\) "OpenAI Codex \(AI agent\)"). CyberArk researchers demonstrated that ChatGPT could be used to create [polymorphic malware](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polymorphic_malware "Polymorphic malware") that could evade security products while requiring little effort by the attacker.[[201]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/ChatGPT#cite_note-Shimony-2023-201)[[202]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/ChatGPT#cite_note-Mascellino-2023-202) From the launch of ChatGPT in the fourth quarter of 2022 to the fourth quarter of 2023, there was a 1,265% increase in malicious [phishing](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Phishing "Phishing") emails and a 967% increase in credential phishing. In an industry survey, cybersecurity professionals argued that it was attributable to cybercriminals' increased use of generative artificial intelligence (including ChatGPT).[[203]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/ChatGPT#cite_note-Violino-2023-203)
In July 2024, _[Futurism](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Futurism_\(website\) "Futurism \(website\)")_ reported that GPT-4o in ChatGPT would sometimes link "scam news sites that deluge the user with fake software updates and virus warnings"; these pop-ups can be used to coerce users into downloading malware or [potentially unwanted programs](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Potentially_unwanted_program "Potentially unwanted program").[[204]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/ChatGPT#cite_note-Dupre-2024-204)
ChatGPT's adoption in education was rapid, but it was initially banned by several institutions. The potential benefits include enhancing personalized learning, improving student productivity, assisting with brainstorming, summarization, and supporting language literacy skills. Students have generally reported positive perceptions, but specific views from educators and students vary widely. Opinions are especially varied on what constitutes appropriate use of ChatGPT in education. Efforts to ban chatbots like ChatGPT in schools focus on preventing cheating, but enforcement faces challenges due to [AI detection](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Artificial_intelligence_content_detection "Artificial intelligence content detection") inaccuracies and widespread accessibility of chatbot technology. In response, many educators are now exploring ways to thoughtfully integrate generative AI into assessments.[[205]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/ChatGPT#cite_note-ChatGPT_in_education_Singer-2023-205)[[206]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/ChatGPT#cite_note-ChatGPT_in_education_Serrano-2023-206)
During the first three months after ChatGPT became available to the public, hundreds of books appeared on [Amazon](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Amazon_\(company\) "Amazon \(company\)") that listed it as author or co-author and featured illustrations made by other AI models such as [Midjourney](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Midjourney "Midjourney").[[207]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/ChatGPT#cite_note-207)[[208]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/ChatGPT#cite_note-208) [Irene Solaiman](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Irene_Solaiman "Irene Solaiman") said she was worried about increased [Anglocentrism](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Anglocentrism "Anglocentrism").[[209]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/ChatGPT#cite_note-Sigal1-209)
Between March and April 2023, _[Il Foglio](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Il_Foglio "Il Foglio")_ published one ChatGPT-generated article a day on its website, hosting a special contest for its readers in the process.[[210]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/ChatGPT#cite_note-210)
In June 2023, hundreds of people attended a "ChatGPT-powered church service" at St. Paul's Church in [Fürth](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/F%C3%BCrth "Fürth"), Germany. Theologian and philosopher Jonas Simmerlein, who presided, said that it was "about 98 percent from the machine".[[211]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/ChatGPT#cite_note-211)[[212]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/ChatGPT#cite_note-212) The ChatGPT-generated avatar told the people, "Dear friends, it is an honor for me to stand here and preach to you as the first artificial intelligence at this year's convention of Protestants in Germany". Reactions to the ceremony were mixed.[[213]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/ChatGPT#cite_note-213)
_[The Last Screenwriter](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Last_Screenwriter "The Last Screenwriter")_ , a 2024 film created and directed by [Peter Luisi](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Peter_Luisi "Peter Luisi"), was written using ChatGPT, and was marketed as "the first film written entirely by AI".[[214]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/ChatGPT#cite_note-214)
_[The Guardian](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Guardian "The Guardian")_ questioned whether any content found on the Internet after ChatGPT's release "can be truly trusted" and called for government regulation.[[215]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/ChatGPT#cite_note-guard20222-215) This has led to concern over the rise of [AI slop](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/AI_slop "AI slop") whereby "meaningless content and writing thereby becomes part of our culture, particularly on social media, which we nonetheless try to understand or fit into our existing cultural horizon."[[216]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/ChatGPT#cite_note-216)
Many companies adopted ChatGPT and similar chatbot technologies into their product offers. In 2023, these changes yielded significant increases in company valuations.[[217]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/ChatGPT#cite_note-217)[[218]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/ChatGPT#cite_note-RSurge1-218) Reuters attributed this surge to ChatGPT's role in turning [AI](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Artificial_intelligence "Artificial intelligence") into [Wall Street](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wall_Street "Wall Street")'s buzzword.[[218]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/ChatGPT#cite_note-RSurge1-218) Despite decades of using AI, Wall Street professionals report that consistently beating the market with AI, including recent large language models, is challenging due to limited and noisy financial data.[[219]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/ChatGPT#cite_note-219)
ChatGPT can provide health information to users[[220]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/ChatGPT#cite_note-220) and assist professionals with diagnosis and staying up to date with clinical guidelines.[[221]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/ChatGPT#cite_note-medical_bundle_1-221) It can be used to summarize medical journal articles for researchers. In medical education, it can explain concepts, generate case scenarios, and be used by students preparing for licensing examinations.[[222]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/ChatGPT#cite_note-medmeta2024-222)
A February 2023 study in _[PLOS Digital Health](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/PLOS_Digital_Health "PLOS Digital Health")_ found that ChatGPT 3.5 was capable of passing the [United States Medical Licensing Examination](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_States_Medical_Licensing_Examination "United States Medical Licensing Examination").[[223]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/ChatGPT#cite_note-223)[[224]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/ChatGPT#cite_note-DePeau-Wilson-2023-224) ChatGPT has also passed the Specialty Certificate Examination in Dermatology.[[221]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/ChatGPT#cite_note-medical_bundle_1-221)
However, ChatGPT shows inconsistent responses, lack of specificity, lack of control over patient data, and a limited ability to take additional context (such as regional variations) into consideration.[[222]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/ChatGPT#cite_note-medmeta2024-222)[[225]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/ChatGPT#cite_note-225) The [hallucinations](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hallucination_\(artificial_intelligence\) "Hallucination \(artificial intelligence\)") characteristic of LLMs pose particular danger in medical contexts, and ChatGPT's ability to come up with false or faulty citations was highly criticized.[[222]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/ChatGPT#cite_note-medmeta2024-222)[[226]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/ChatGPT#cite_note-226) According to a 2024 study in the _[International Journal of Surgery](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/International_Journal_of_Surgery "International Journal of Surgery")_ , concerns include "research fraud, lack of originality, ethics, copyright, legal difficulties".[[222]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/ChatGPT#cite_note-medmeta2024-222)
According to a September 2025 article in _[Lancet Psychiatry](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lancet_Psychiatry "Lancet Psychiatry")_ , many individuals use ChatGPT and comparable chatbots for mental health and emotional support despite a warning from OpenAI against using ChatGPT as a therapist. The study notes a lack of research on efficacy, poor consistency in dangerous situations, limited regulation and liability, and poor transparency from OpenAI.[[227]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/ChatGPT#cite_note-227)
A July 2025 study in the journal _Digital Health_ found that users reported employing ChatGPT to manage mental health concerns "due to perceived therapist-like qualities (e.g. emotional support, accurate understanding, and constructive feedback) and machine-like benefits (e.g. constant availability, expansive cognitive capacity, lack of negative reactions, and perceived objectivity)." The study calls for improved [AI literacy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/AI_literacy "AI literacy") and mandatory disclosure from AI providers to address ethical concerns such as privacy, bias, the lack of liability, and emotional over-reliance.[[228]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/ChatGPT#cite_note-228)
